Key concept Mark-Making as Meaning

Breaking Typographic Conventions

Approach to Type on Path:

Ed Fella's work blends vernacular influences, folk art aesthetics, and expressive mark-making to create unique typographic landscapes. His hand-drawn letterforms often follow irregular paths and challenge traditional notions of legibility while still communicating effectively.

Fella's work demonstrates how type can function simultaneously as both language and visual form, showing that the path itself becomes part of the message.

Expressive Qualities of Type on Path

When placing type on a path, consider what the shape expresses:

  • Curves: Suggest movement, flow, organic qualities
  • Straight lines: Communicate structure, stability, formality
  • Circular paths: Can represent unity, completion, continuity
  • Character choice: Affects the texture and emotional tone (angular vs. flowing)

Ask yourself: How does the path shape and character selection reinforce the content's meaning?

Technique 2: Keyboard Mark-Making

üëÅÔ∏è WATCH DEMONSTRATION

David Carson

Revolutionized graphic design in the 1990s with his experimental typography in Ray Gun magazine

Typographic Disruption

Approach to Mark-Making:

Carson's work is characterized by intuitive, non-grid layouts, distorted type, and layered compositions that prioritize emotional response over traditional legibility. His willingness to deconstruct letterforms and create new visual languages from typographic elements demonstrates the potential of mark-making approaches.

Carson's keyboard mark-making often creates rhythmic patterns that guide the eye in unconventional ways, challenging how we read and process information.

Expressive Qualities of Keyboard Marks

Different characters create different visual effects and can convey specific qualities:

  • / (slashes): Dynamic, energetic, suggest movement
  • = (equals): Structured, methodical, stable
  • ~ (tildes): Flowing, undulating, gentle
  • | (vertical bars): Strong, authoritative, rigid
  • * (asterisks): Playful, sparkly, attention-grabbing

The density, spacing, and arrangement of your marks further influences their expressive quality.

Creating Meaning with Symbol Brushes

Symbol brushes can express specific qualities through their form and arrangement:

  • Direction: Establishes flow and movement through your design
  • Density: Creates areas of visual weight and emphasis
  • Rhythm: Regular or irregular spacing affects the tempo and mood
  • Scale variation: Can suggest depth, hierarchy, or transformation

Consider how your symbol brushes interact with content elements to reinforce or contrast with their meaning.

Expressive Quality of Hand-Drawn Elements

Hand-drawn marks add a human touch and personality that can convey specific qualities:

  • Texture: Adds tactile quality and material sensibility
  • Imperfection: Creates authenticity and approachability
  • Uniqueness: Distinguishes your work with personal expression

Even simple hand-drawn marks can carry deep expressive meaning through their quality and character.
